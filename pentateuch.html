Pentateuch
Book Key Chapters Main Characters Main Events Key Themes
Genesis 1-3, 6-8, 12, 32, 40-43, 45 Adam and Eve, Noah, Abraham and Sarah, Joseph, God, Jacob, and Isaac God Creates Heaven and Earth, Adam and Eve eat the forbidden apple Creation, Fall, Redemption, the coming of Jesus of Nazareth/New Covenant
Exodus 3-15, 20-24, 31-35 God, Moses, the Israelites, Aaron, Pharoh, Jacob, and Joseph The ten plagues, the Passover, the parting of the Red Sea, the fearsome majesty of God’s presence at Mount Sinai, the giving of the Ten Commandments, the building of the tabernacle Covenant Promises, Covenant mediator, Covenant Presence, fulfillment of God’s promises to the patriarchs.
Leviticus 4, 5, 6, 7, 11-23 Israelite Priests, Moses, Aaron, Nadab, Abihu, and God God gives instructions on offerings; God instructs people how to live and act The Holy Lord is present among his people, to approach God followers must be wholehearted in their devotion, Atonement is a gracious act of God.
Numbers 10, 11, 12, 20, 21 Moses, Aaron, Miriam, Joshua, Caleb, Eleazar, Korah, and Balaam The Israelites did not trust God and did not take the promise land, Moses acted as God and did not make water from the rock and was condemned to die in the desert. Do not rebel against God, do not place yourself in God’s shoes, trust God’s will always.
Deuteronomy 4-8, 29, 30, 34 God, Moses, and Joshua The Israelites revert their ways and go back to following the ten commandments, Moses Dies Election, faithfulness, obedience, and Yahweh's promise of blessings
Looking Down The first five books of the Bible illustrate the creation, fall, redemption theme we see in the rest of the Bible. In addition to creation, fall, and rise we see how God wants the best for his people and instructs them on every basis of life and how to act. We see God’s fairness and love for his creation, but we also see God’s wrath when we as human’s have sinned and disobeyed his direct orders.
Looking Foward Like what I said above, the Pentateuch tells us the story of the Bible for what’s to come. The new covenant, the one of Jesus Christ who died for our sins.
