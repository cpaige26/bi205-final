Prophetic Books
Book Key Chapters Main Characters Main Events Key Themes
Isaiah 1-3, 6, 9-10, 27, 34, 41, 43-44, 46-48, 57, 59 Isaiah and his sons, Shear-Jashub and Maher-Shalal-Hash-Baz Uzziah dies; Isaiah’s call, Assyrian invasion, Sennacherib’s death, Babylonian’s to destroy Jerusalem, Israel to return from Babylonian exile Salvation is the overarching theme in the book of Isaiah. Other themes include judgment, holiness, punishment, captivity, the fall of the nation, comfort, hope, and salvation through the coming Messiah
Jeremiah 2-8, 10-17, 20-23, 26, 31, 33, 35, 39-42, 44, 46-52 Jeremiah, Baruch, King Josiah, King Jehoiakim, Ebed-Melech, King Nebuchadnezzar, the Recabite people Jeremiah is persecuted and suffers, Jerusalem sins and falls Obedience is a key theme, because Jerusalem sinned, the nation was eventually destroyed because no one obeyed God
Lamentations 2, 5 People of the City God destroyed the city God’s wrath and God’s mercies
Ezekial 4-10, 12-16, 20-22 Ezekiel, Israel's leaders, Ezekiel's wife, and King Nebuchadnezzar Judgement against Idolatry, Idolaters are killed, non-Godly people are condemned Ezekial was called to be a “watchman for God” to warn everyone the dangers that came with committing idolatry
Daniel 2-6, 10 Daniel, Nebuchadnezzar, God, Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego Daniel interprets Nebuchadnezzar’s dream, God saves Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego from death in the furnace, Nebuchadnezzar praises God. Daniel interprets dreams, Daniel is thrown into the lion’s den, Daniel has visions Only God can deliver you to safety
Hosea 2-5, 9-11, 13-14 Hosea, Gomer, Jezreel, People of Israel Broken marriage symbolizes broken covenant, Israel is unfaithful, and God is compassionate Hosea taught that to love God is to love the neighbor and to refrain from doing evil. He also proclaimed that the love of gods other than the Lord led people to commit acts of injustice and oppression
Joel 1-3 Joel Joel prophesies the coming of a locust swarm, and foretells of an army from the North who will destroy their land, and begs the people to repent to God God is just, punishing sin, but also merciful to those who repent
Amos 2-5, 9 Amos Amos preaches that Israel has committed the same sins as the neighboring countries, and is not better than them God’s anger, Judgement, and Prophetic authority are all themes emphasized in Amos
Obadiah 1 Obadiah¸ Lord Pronounces judgement on Edom The destructive power of pride is a theme found in Obadiah
Jonah 1-4 Jonah, People of Nineveh God commands Jonah and Jonah disobeys, God spares Nineveh God has patience and mercy
Micah 1-3, 5 Micah Prophecy of Jesus Christ God’s control and Justice are key themes to the story of Micah
Nahum 1-3 Nahum Invasion of Nineveh God's wrath
Habakkuk 1, 3 Habakkuk None God's power, the wicked will suffer, and the righteous will live by faith
Zephaniah 1-3 Zephaniah Judgement upon Judah Seek the Lord
Haggai 1-2 Haggai, Zerubbabel The House of the Lord began Construction God judges the unclean
Zechariah 1-3, 9-14 Zechariah Construction of the Temple God rewards those who are obediant to him
Malachi 1-4 Malachi Israelites sin and disobey the covenant God loves his people
Looking Back This section of the Bible is all about showing how God’s people have failed again and again but that he loves us so much that if we repent and ask for forgiveness, he grants it to us
Looking Forward With these being the last books of the Old Testament, God was showing his people how merciful he can be, now with the start of the New Testament or New Covenant/Promise we will see Jesus and how much God cares for us that he sent Jesus to live on this world
