For example, I found this video and found it hitting close to home because I rather enjoy living in comfort. But in order to grow I need to get up and out of my comfort zone. Here are some principles, sources, and implications I found in the video.

Principal Source Implication
God uses setbacks and discomforts in order to help us grow as Christians 1 Peter 5:10 By being fired, Bill was put in a position where the pain and discomfort made him stronger
Christians who become comfortable in this world are sinning 2nd Corinthians 4:4 Because Christian's become so comfortable in worldly possessions it can limit your spiritual growth, we have got to get out of our comfort zones in order to grow
The enviorment in which you place yourself whether it be where you work, live, or pray will dictate your growth Proverbs 13:20 Throughout the Bible, there is a call for believers to surround themselves with other believers, this will allow you to grow more spiritually. There is also a call to avoid surrounding yourself with sinners because by doing so you will surely backslide
God is the furthest thing from chaos Psalm 135:6 According to the video, chaos is defined as, "having zero predictability or control over inputs and outcomes." However, we know that from the Bible God is all-knowning, he knows everything past, present, and future. If anyone is in control it is God.
Someone can help you get you to a uncomfortable stage in order to grow Proverbs 27:17 Mentors are a necessity in order to grow. These mentors can be parents, friends, teachers, leaders in the church, or bosses. "When people are on their own, people will consciously or subconsciously choose the comfort of order and they need to be pushed into complexity in order to grow." Christians need to strive to push other Christian and non-believers to learn of God.
